Abstract:

The most important feature in the mobile environment is mobility itself. It enables us to be in contact while we are outside the reach of conventional communication spaces. Mobility can be conceptualised in different ways, i.e., mobility of the user, mobility of the device, and mobility of services, and these three aspects should be addressed both technically and contextually.

What do we all have in common?

Photo Credit: d_vdm cc
We don't simply own phones, we own advanced computers that the Geeks of the 80's never could have dreamed of. We carry them in our pockets and our phones know everything about us, our friends, where we are, where we have been. They contain powerfull sensors and high resolution screens. And above all we almost never leave home without one.

You (often) interact differently on each device

  • Your read texts on your mobile while traveling
  • You write on you laptop
  • You watch youtube on your tablet
  • You game on your gaming console
What fits perfectly in one place, doesn’t necessarily fit somewhere else. Whatever device is available thats the device you are going to use for that task.

Learners and their phones have a personal relationship

Bootstrap and the Moodle Bootstrap theme

  • The Bootstrap framework was built by @fat and @mdo
  • David Scotson and myself developed the Moodle Bootstrap theme
  • Then it got adopted into Moodle core (Bootstrapbase)
  • And now it's going to be the default theme for Moodle
Bootstrap is not the only CSS framework out there, but is definately the most popular framework with many site using it and many ways of customizing it.
